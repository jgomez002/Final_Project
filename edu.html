The Digital Divide

What is the digital divide?

The gap between demographics and regions that have access to modern information and communications technology and those that don't or have restricted access


This Digital Gap disproportionately effects those belonging to marginalized communities. Immigrants, People of Color, Low-Income, Disabled, Neurodivergent for example. While we are quite accustomed to the indicators for a url, login formats, mouse movement, double clicking for more options, normalized UX.UI convenction these marginalized communities are not

The internet shouldn't be this way

Being strongly highlighted during the COVID-19 Pandemic, our reliances on the internet and digital world. With the initial lockdowns in 2020, it felt as the world switched to be entirely online. Those with steady access to stable internet, proficient digital literacy skills and digital problem solving skills had an easier transition. Those who lacked to qualities struggled immensely.

COVID-19 began to highlight the issues low income POC and immigrants had navigating this digital age for decades. This issues the digital divide. This divide reveals the classism and digital inequalities that are so normalized in the digital world and UX/UI design.



While recognizing the digital divide in these digitally reliant times, is an important step to create more accessible online navigation and UX/UI we must put effort to really understand the experience that many low-income immigrants and POC have while navigating the digital world.
